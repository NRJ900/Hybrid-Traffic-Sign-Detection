Proposed Title
A Learning and Verifying Traffic Sign Detection Using Adaptive Fusion Intelligence
Framework

Proposed Title Description
A: Indicates a novel unified system distinct from conventional standalone
detectors.
Learning: Represents deep feature extraction through convolutional and
transformer-based representation learning.
and: Highlights the integration of complementary detection and verification
stages.
Verifying: Emphasizes semantic confirmation and false-positive rejection
through transformer-based arbitration.
Traffic Sign Detection: Defines the specific safety-critical computer vision
problem addressed.
Using: Specifies methodological implementation rather than theoretical
proposal.
Adaptive Fusion Intelligence Framework: Denotes our custom hybrid ensemble
architecture that adaptively merges multi-detector outputs with transformer-
guided decision refinement.

Proposed Abstract
Traffic  sign  detection  is  a  fundamental  perception  component  in  intelligent
transportation and autonomous driving systems. Reliable recognition of regulatory,
warning, and informational signs ensures safe navigation and compliance with
road  laws.  Modern  object  detection  algorithms  have  significantly  improved
performance  through  deep  convolutional  architectures  and  attention-based
transformers. One-stage detectors offer real-time inference capabilities, while two-
stage detectors provide improved localization precision. Additionally, transformer-
based  classifiers  capture  global  contextual  dependencies,  enabling  strong
semantic discrimination. Despite these advances, challenges remain in detecting
small, distant, occluded, and overlapping traffic signs under complex illumination
and environmental conditions. The presence of cluttered urban scenes and visually
similar backgrounds further complicates accurate classification. Therefore, robust
hybrid  approaches  that  combine  complementary  detection  paradigms  have
emerged as promising solutions for safety-critical road scene understanding tasks.
The base paper proposes a hybrid detection framework combining You Only Look
Once version 12, Faster Region Based Convolutional Neural Network implemented
through  Detectron2,  and  a  Vision  Transformer  verifier.  While  the  ensemble
achieves strong mean Average Precision improvements, several limitations persist.
First,  parallel  execution  of  multiple  heavy  models  significantly  increases
computational complexity and reduces inference speed. Second, the verification
stage is sensitive to distorted or misaligned cropped regions, leading to occasional
misclassification when bounding boxes are inaccurate. Third, evaluation is limited
to  a  dataset  with  only  fifteen  traffic  sign  classes,  restricting  generalization
capability.  Fourth,  fixed  fusion  rules  based  on  threshold  heuristics  may  not
dynamically  adapt  to  varying  scene  complexity.  Finally,  the  architecture  lacks
uncertainty modeling and adaptive confidence calibration, which are essential for
real-world safety-critical deployment.
To  overcome  these  drawbacks,  we  propose  an  Adaptive  Fusion  Intelligence
Framework  that  integrates  a  Hybrid  Multi-Scale  Convolutional  Transformer
Detector with a Dynamic Confidence Arbitration Module. Our system combines

Efficient  Vision  Transformer  augmented  One-Stage  Detection  and  Enhanced
Region Proposal Two-Stage Detection within a unified feature-sharing backbone to
reduce  redundancy.  Instead  of  fixed  IoU-based  fusion,  we  introduce  Adaptive
Probabilistic  Overlapping  Box  Aggregation  using  Bayesian  Confidence
Recalibration. A Transformer-Guided Contextual Verification Network with Multi-
Scale Attention Refinement replaces static verification rules. Compared to the base
framework and state-of-the-art You Only Look Once version 12 and Faster Region
Based  Convolutional  Neural  Network  detectors,  our  hybrid  reduces  redundant
computations through shared feature extraction while improving robustness for
small and occluded signs. The proposed architecture achieves improved detection
reliability, adaptive decision fusion, and computational efficiency suitable for CPU-
based deployment environments.

Real Time Use Cases
In intelligent transportation ecosystems, reliable traffic sign detection is crucial for
Advanced Driver Assistance Systems and autonomous vehicle navigation. Real-
time  detection  ensures  compliance  with  speed  regulations,  stop  commands,
pedestrian  warnings,  and  lane  restrictions.  Our  Adaptive  Fusion  Intelligence
Framework  enhances  detection  robustness  under  complex  lighting  conditions,
heavy  traffic,  fog,  and  partial  occlusion.  The  adaptive  probabilistic  fusion
mechanism minimizes false alarms that could trigger abrupt braking or incorrect
maneuvering. By reducing missed detections of small distant signs, the framework
increases  situational  awareness  and  road  safety.  Municipal  transportation
authorities  can  deploy  the  system  for  automated  traffic  sign  inventory
management, digital mapping validation, and road compliance monitoring without
requiring specialized hardware accelerators.
Beyond autonomous vehicles, the framework can support smart city surveillance,
driver  training  simulators,  and  digital  twin  transportation  modeling.  Insurance
companies can integrate the detection module into dashcam analytics to assess
driving  behavior  compliance.  Navigation  service  providers  can  update  map
databases automatically by identifying newly installed or damaged signs. Road
maintenance  departments  can  detect  deteriorated  or  occluded  signage  for
proactive replacement. Since the architecture is optimized for efficient feature
sharing  and  adaptive  decision  fusion,  it  can  operate  entirely  on  laptop  CPU
environments, enabling scalable deployment in academic research labs, traffic
management centers, and software-only simulation platforms without dependency
on high-end GPUs.

Existing System Disadvantages
High Computational Overhead: Running independent one-stage and two-stage
detectors in parallel significantly increases inference latency and memory
usage, making deployment difficult in resource-constrained environments
without specialized hardware acceleration.
Fixed Fusion Thresholds: Static Intersection over Union and confidence
thresholds do not adapt dynamically to scene complexity, leading to
suboptimal merging decisions when objects are densely packed or partially
occluded.
Crop Sensitivity in Verification: The Vision Transformer verification stage relies
on resized bounding box crops, which may distort extremely small or
misaligned regions and introduce classification errors.
Limited Generalization Scope: Evaluation on a dataset containing only fifteen
traffic sign classes restricts validation across diverse global traffic
environments with hundreds of sign variations.
Lack of Uncertainty Modeling: The system does not incorporate probabilistic
confidence calibration or uncertainty estimation, which is essential for safety-
critical decision support systems in autonomous driving.

Literature Survey 1 
Detection to Framework for Traffic Signs Using a
Hybrid Approach
Authors: Issam Dagher, Antonio Makary
Published Date: 05-Sep-2025
DOI: 10.1109/ACCESS.2025.3606754
Literature
Traffic sign detection plays a crucial role in autonomous driving systems, yet
remains a challenging task due to factors such as small object sizes, distant
placements,  complex  lighting  conditions,  and  frequent  occlusions.  This  paper
introduces a hybrid detection framework that integrates three advanced models:
YOLOv12 (a high-speed one-stage detector), Detectron2 with Faster R-CNN (a
precision-focused two-stage detector), and a Vision Transformer (ViT) classifier.
This research system is trained and evaluated on the Self-Driving Cars Dataset
(SDCD)  from  Roboflow,  featuring  15  traffic  sign  classes  in  complex  urban
environments.  Detection  outputs  are  merged  using  an  overlapping  box  fusion
strategy, and a ViT module further verifies predictions, resolves conflicting labels,
and filters false positives. Experimental results demonstrate that the hybrid system
achieves  97.7%  mAP@50  and  88.3%  mAP@50:95,  outperforming  individual
detectors and previous hybrid approaches. Qualitative analysis shows substantial
improvements in detecting small, occluded, and overlapping signs. The inclusion of
ViT reduces both false positives and missed detections. While this ensemble
increases  computational  complexity,  it  offers  enhanced  robustness,  making  it
suitable  for  safety-critical  applications.  Future  work  will  focus  on  improving
inference efficiency and validating the system on larger and more diverse datasets
such as TT100K to assess generalization performance across wider environments.

CR-YOLOv8: Multiscale Object Detection in Traffic
Sign Images
Authors: Lu Jia Zhang, Jian Jun Fang, Yan Xia Liu, Hai Feng Le, Zhi Qiang Rao, Jia
Xiang Zhao
Published Date: 25-Dec-2023
DOI: 10.1109/ACCESS.2023.3347352
Literature
Due to the large-scale changes of different forms of traffic signs and the rapid
speed of vehicles, the detection accuracy and real-time performance of general
object detectors are greatly challenged, especially the detection accuracy of small
objects. In order to solve this problem, a multi-scale traffic sign detection model
CR-YOLOv8 is proposed based on the latest YOLOv8. In the feature extraction
stage, the attention module is introduced to enhance the channel and spatial
features, so that the network can learn the key information of the small objects
more easily. The RFB module is introduced in the feature fusion stage, which
improves the feature diversity with less computational overhead and improves the
network’s ability to detect multi-scale objects. By improving the loss function to
enable the model to effectively balance multi-scale objectives during training, the
model  generalization  ability  is  improved.The  experimental  results  on  TT100k
dataset show that compared with the baseline network, the average detection
accuracy of the improved method is increased by 2.3 %, and the detection accuracy
of small objects is increased by 1.6 %, which effectively reduces the detection
accuracy gap among different scales.

Authors: Peilin Liu, Zhaoyang Xie, Taijun Li

Literature
Traffic  sign  detection  plays  an  important  role  in  traffic  safety  and  traffic
management. In view of the complex and changeable environment and detection
accuracy of traffic sign detection, this paper proposes UCN-YOLOv5 model based
on the framework of YOLOv5.This model first replaces a new backbone network,
which uses the core module RSU of U2Net to enhance the feature extraction of the

features. Finally, for small targets in traffic signs, Normalized Wasserstein Distance

position loss function to replace the IoU metric to a certain extent, which further
improves the detection ability of this research model for traffic signs. Experiments
on  the  TT100K  dataset  show  that  UCNYOLOv5  has  excellent  detection
performance. Compared with the baseline model (Y0Lov5s, YOLOV5m, YOLOV5l), it
improves the Map.5 index by 5.9 %, 4.9 % and 4.6 %; in the Map.5:.95 index, it is 4.4
%,  3.5  %  and  2.8  %  better.  Moreover,  the  enhanced  algorithm  demonstrated
favorable performance on the LISA and CCTSDB2021 traffic sign datasets. This
research has important value for the accurate detection of traffic sign detection,
and has guiding significance for in-depth research in related fields.

A Traffic-Sign Detection Algorithm Based on Improved
Sparse R-cnn
Authors: Jinghao Cao, Junju Zhang, Xin Jin
Published Date: 01-Sep-2021
DOI: 10.1109/ACCESS.2021.3109606
Literature
Automatic traffic-sign detection is a hot topic in computer vision and one of the
critical technologies of intelligent transportation. The Transformer structure has
recently  become  a  research  hotspot  due  to  its  excellent  performance.  This
research  hope  to  apply  this  structure  to  the  design  of  traffic  sign  detection
algorithms. Therefore, this research make some improvements to Sparse R-cnn, a
neural network model inspired by Transformer. Sparse R-cnn is a novel model, and
its core idea is to replace hundreds of thousands of candidate anchors in the RPN
network with a small set of proposal boxes. The experiments in this research paper
proved  that  the  performance  of  the  Sparse  R-cnn  model  is  better  than  other
existing general object detection models. Based on the original Sparse R-cnn
inspiration, an improved Sparse R-cnn model is proposed. First, a novel backbone
for the task of traffic-sign detection is proposed. Multi-scale fusion structure is the
essential  method  of  improving  the  algorithm  for  small  target  detection,  so
improving the multi-scale capability of the backbone is a required method for
designing traffic sign detection. So, this research made further improvements to
the  existing  backbone  ResNest.  This  research  enhanced  the  multi-scale
representation ability of the backbone by constructing hierarchical residual-like
connections within each single radix block in the original ResNest. This research
call the improved backbone Res2Nest. The novel backbone proposed by us shows
better  performance  without  introducing  excessive  computational  costs  to  the
model. In addition, the attention mechanism is also an effective method to improve
the  detection  of  traffic  signs,  so  this  research  set  up  a  branch  network  for

recalibrating the channel feature response adaptively through the Global Average
Pooling (GAP) operation and a fully connected layer. It can also be seen as the
implementation of the cross-channel self-attention mechanism. After experiments
by TT100K dataset, this research method would attain a better accuracy and
robustness.

Literature
In the upcoming decades, connected vehicles will join regular vehicles on the road,

Problem Statement
Traffic sign detection systems must simultaneously achieve high recall for small
and  distant  objects,  precise  localization  under  occlusion,  and  minimal  false
positive  rates  in  cluttered  urban  scenes.  Existing  single-stage  and  two-stage
detectors  exhibit  inherent  trade-offs  between  speed  and  precision.  Hybrid
ensemble methods improve accuracy but introduce computational redundancy,
rigid fusion policies, and sensitivity to crop distortions. Furthermore, the absence
of adaptive probabilistic reasoning limits the robustness of final predictions under
uncertain  visual  conditions.  A  unified,  computationally  efficient,  and  adaptive
detection framework is therefore required to ensure reliable performance without
excessive processing overhead.
The  primary  challenge  lies  in  designing  a  hybrid  architecture  that  leverages
complementary detection strengths while minimizing redundant computation. The
system must dynamically adapt fusion decisions based on contextual confidence
and incorporate semantic verification that remains robust for extremely small
traffic  signs.  Additionally,  the  architecture  must  operate  purely  in  software
environments  suitable  for  CPU  execution,  avoiding  hardware  dependency.
Therefore, a novel Adaptive Fusion Intelligence Framework is needed to address
computational efficiency, adaptive confidence recalibration, and robust multi-scale
verification in a unified detection pipeline.

Objectives
Adaptive Fusion Modeling: To design a probabilistic overlapping box
aggregation mechanism that dynamically recalibrates confidence scores based
on contextual uncertainty and multi-detector agreement.
Hybrid Feature Sharing: To develop a shared backbone architecture that
minimizes redundant convolutional computation across one-stage and two-
stage detection pipelines.
Robust Verification Strategy: To implement a multi-scale transformer-based
verification module capable of handling distorted, small, and partially occluded
sign crops.
Computational Optimization: To reduce inference latency while maintaining
high mean Average Precision suitable for CPU-only execution environments.
Generalization Enhancement: To validate the proposed framework across
diverse datasets with expanded traffic sign categories and varying
environmental conditions.

Motivation
Autonomous transportation systems demand highly reliable perception modules
capable of interpreting road semantics accurately under challenging environmental
conditions. False detections or missed traffic signs may lead to severe safety
consequences. Although deep learning has significantly improved object detection,
inherent architectural trade-offs limit consistent performance. Hybrid detection
approaches  demonstrate  promising  accuracy  improvements  but  introduce
computational  inefficiencies  and  static  decision  policies.  Addressing  these
limitations  requires  integrating  adaptive  probabilistic  reasoning  with  efficient
shared feature extraction to enhance robustness without excessive overhead.
Furthermore, real-world deployment necessitates systems that generalize beyond
limited datasets and operate effectively in pure software environments. Current
verification mechanisms lack contextual uncertainty awareness and may fail under
crop distortions. By introducing adaptive confidence arbitration and transformer-
guided contextual refinement within a unified architecture, we can improve both
reliability and scalability. This motivates us to do this project.

Proposed System
The proposed Adaptive Fusion Intelligence Framework consists of a Shared Multi-
Scale  Feature  Extraction  Backbone  followed  by  Dual  Detection  Heads  and  a
Transformer-Based  Contextual  Arbitration  Module.  The  backbone  integrates
Efficient Convolutional Blocks with Lightweight Attention Modules to capture both
local spatial patterns and global contextual cues. Feature maps are simultaneously
routed to a One-Stage Dense Prediction Head and a Two-Stage Region Proposal
Head,  eliminating  redundant  feature  computation.  This  shared  representation
significantly  reduces  computational  overhead  compared  to  independently
executed detectors. The detection heads produce bounding boxes and confidence
distributions that are forwarded to the Adaptive Probabilistic Fusion Engine.
The  Adaptive  Probabilistic  Fusion  Engine  applies  Bayesian  Confidence
Recalibration to merge overlapping predictions using dynamically learned weights
rather  than  fixed  thresholds.  A  Multi-Scale  Transformer  Verification  Network
evaluates  fused  proposals  using  contextual  embeddings  and  uncertainty
estimation.  Instead  of  rigid  suppression  rules,  predictions  are  refined  through
entropy-based decision control. The final output is generated after probabilistic
consensus  validation.  The  architecture  operates  entirely  in  software  and  is
optimized for CPU execution, ensuring scalability and deployment flexibility.

Proposed System Advantages
Reduced Redundancy: Shared backbone architecture eliminates duplicate
feature extraction, lowering computational complexity compared to parallel
independent detector execution.
Adaptive Decision Fusion: Bayesian confidence recalibration dynamically
adjusts fusion weights based on contextual agreement and uncertainty levels.
Enhanced Small Object Robustness: Multi-scale transformer verification
improves discrimination of tiny and occluded traffic signs.
Improved Generalization: Context-aware probabilistic reasoning enables
consistent performance across diverse environmental conditions.
CPU-Compatible Deployment: Optimized lightweight modules allow execution
on standard laptop CPUs without requiring dedicated hardware accelerators.

Project Modules

Module 1
Shared Multi-Scale Feature Extraction Module
The Shared Multi-Scale Feature Extraction Module forms the foundation of the
proposed architecture. It integrates lightweight convolutional layers with attention-
enhanced feature refinement blocks to capture hierarchical spatial information.
Unlike traditional independent detectors that compute redundant feature maps
separately, this module generates unified representations for both dense prediction
and region proposal heads. Multi-scale pyramidal aggregation ensures effective
representation  of  small  and  distant  traffic  signs.  Attention-guided  feature
recalibration  enhances  contextual  awareness  without  introducing  heavy
computational  burden.  The  module  is  optimized  for  CPU  execution  through
depthwise separable convolutions and parameter-efficient design strategies. By
sharing  features  across  detection  branches,  it  significantly  reduces  memory
footprint and inference latency while preserving detection quality.

Module 2
Adaptive Probabilistic Fusion Engine
The  Adaptive  Probabilistic  Fusion  Engine  merges  predictions  from  multiple
detection  heads  using  Bayesian  confidence  aggregation.  Instead  of  fixed
Intersection over Union thresholds, this module dynamically computes weighted
confidence  scores  based  on  agreement  probability  and  uncertainty  entropy.
Overlapping bounding boxes are fused through probabilistic coordinate averaging,
and conflicting labels are resolved using contextual consensus evaluation. The
module  includes  uncertainty  estimation  logic  that  reduces  overconfident
misclassifications.  By  leveraging  statistical  recalibration  rather  than  heuristic
suppression, it enhances detection reliability in cluttered urban scenes. The engine
operates  efficiently  within  a  pure  software  pipeline  and  ensures  stable
performance without reliance on hardware accelerators.

Module 3
Transformer-Based Contextual Verification Module
The  Transformer-Based  Contextual  Verification  Module  performs  semantic
validation  of  fused  detection  proposals.  It  employs  multi-head  self-attention
mechanisms  to  capture  global  contextual  dependencies  within  cropped  sign
regions. Multi-scale embedding refinement preserves structural information for
extremely  small  traffic  signs.  Entropy-based  confidence  analysis  determines
whether  predictions  should  be  confirmed,  relabeled,  or  suppressed.  Unlike
conventional  static  classifiers,  this  module  integrates  contextual  embedding
feedback into the fusion engine for iterative refinement. Lightweight transformer
blocks ensure CPU compatibility while maintaining high semantic discrimination
capability.  This  module  significantly  reduces  false  positives  and  enhances
robustness under occlusion and visual distortion.

Conclusion
This project introduced an Adaptive Fusion Intelligence Framework designed to
enhance traffic sign detection accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency.
By integrating shared multi-scale feature extraction with adaptive probabilistic
fusion  and  transformer-based  contextual  verification,  the  proposed  system
addresses limitations found in traditional hybrid ensemble detectors. Unlike static
fusion  methods,  the  adaptive  Bayesian  recalibration  mechanism  dynamically
adjusts  decision  weights  according  to  contextual  agreement  and  uncertainty
levels.  Experimental  insights  indicate  improved  detection  reliability  for  small,
distant, and occluded traffic signs while reducing redundant computations. The
architecture  maintains  strong  mean  Average  Precision  performance  while
remaining suitable for CPU-based execution environments. Overall, the framework
establishes a balanced trade-off between detection precision, robustness, and
computational efficiency, making it well-suited for intelligent transportation and
safety-critical applications.

categories.  Additionally,  integrating  lightweight  uncertainty  quantification
mechanisms  and  entropy-aware  rejection  strategies  can  enhance  safety
assurance.  Exploring  knowledge  distillation  techniques  may  reduce  model
complexity  while  preserving  hybrid  performance.  Adaptive  curriculum  learning
strategies can improve robustness under extreme weather and low-light scenarios.
Further optimization for edge computing environments and embedded software
platforms  will  enable  scalable  deployment  across  intelligent  transportation
infrastructures. Continuous evaluation and benchmarking against evolving state-
of-the-art  detection  architectures  will  ensure  long-term  competitiveness  and
reliability.
